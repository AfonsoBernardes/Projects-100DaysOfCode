About Me

I am Afonso Bernardes, a recently graduate 24-year-old who is aims to enter the tech world as a Python developer. Pursuing my master's degree in mechanical engineering provided me with hands-on experience in multiple fields and technologies. During this time, my curiosity for programming grew alongside my efforts to better understand the intricacies of developing software. My passion for programming is evidenced by my initiative to complement my knowledge beyond my university curriculum. As a self-educated Python aficionado, I had the chance to test my skills by developing a Premier League predictor, employing fundamental machine learning algorithms to predict the outcome of a football match based on match statistics, stored and manipulated using PostgreSQL. The foundations of my technical skills were laid during my time at university. I used Matlab as a general-purpose tool, applying it across multiple domains such as robotics and optimization algorithms. With Java, I was introduced to object-oriented programming concepts when developing an interactive Blackjack simulator. Expanding my competencies beyond my academic endeavours prepared me to tackle diverse challenges, while my academic achievements evidence my technical skills in the field.

As part of my master’s thesis, I was employed in a nine-month internship at a SaaS startup, where I built, trained and fine-tuned a neural network with Python. Resorting to libraries such as PyTorch, NumPy, and Matplotlib, I developed a deep fake detection system, which allowed the company to quickly and precisely prevent fraud attempts during user authentication. In addition, I was able to develop my skills with version control systems in a professional context, in this particular instance using Git. My work during this internship granted me a grade of 18 out of 20 on my thesis, demonstrating my ability to effectively apply technical skills to a professional setting.

As a complement to my degree, my interest in computer science topics led me to enrol in multiple online courses. By articulating theory and graded exercises, I had the chance to deepen my knowledge of data structures and algorithms. I learned about the trade-offs between using one structure versus the other and the cost of performing operations associated with each structure, all this coupled with implementing and stress testing several algorithms, learning about their time complexity. These accomplishments granted me certificates which prove both my ability to learn quickly on my own and my initiative to rise above expectations.

Furthermore, during my apprenticeship, I exercised my communication and problem-solving skills. Working in an Agile environment required me to interact with individuals across multiple departments, compelling me to understand and make myself understood by people from diverse backgrounds. Besides academic and professional achievements, my engagement in extracurricular activities also helped me develop my soft skills. My time management and communication abilities matured during eight years of playing rugby while simultaneously focusing on my studies. In addition, my interest in languages goes beyond programming. My drive and perseverance are corroborated by my B1 certificate, attained after five years of being a French autodidact.
